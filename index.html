Best Study Spots on Campus I Actually Use

My personal go-to places when I need focus, quiet, and a space that helps me lock in.

What I Look For

Focus

I need a place where I can sit down and actually concentrate without constant distractions.

Comfort

If I’m staying for hours, I need decent seating and enough space for my laptop, notes, and everything else.

Convenience

Being close to campus and open late honestly makes a huge difference during busy weeks.

Featured Study Spot: Engineering Hall

Engineering Hall is my top study spot when I really need to focus. The environment feels productive, and being around other engineering students automatically puts me in the right mindset. It’s usually calm without being completely silent, and I can stay there for long study sessions. When I have problem sets, labs, or anything major due, this is the place I trust the most.

More Study Spots I Like

Sencha Café

Sencha Café is one of my favorite off-campus spots because it stays open late and is really close to campus. It’s perfect when the libraries close or when I just need a change of scenery. The relaxed vibe and being able to grab a drink while studying makes long nights feel a little easier.

College Library

College Library is reliable during midterms and finals. There’s always a lot of seating, and it feels like everyone there is locked in, which motivates me to stay focused too.

Heard enough?

If you need serious focus, start with Engineering Hall. If it’s late, head to Sencha.
